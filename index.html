Neerja Bhanot ✈

★ The first and the youngest woman recipient of the Ashoka Chakra Award ★

She died heroically, saving 360 Pan Am Flight 73 passengers from terrorists on September 5th, 1986

Neerja Bhanot (7 September 1963 - 5 September 1986) was a 22 year old Indian senior flight purser on the Empress of the Seas, a Pam Am 73 hijacked at Jinnah International Airport, Karanchi by four armed men en route to Frankfurt and New York. During the 17-hour ordeal, she helped three-member cockpit crew escape in order to ground the plane so it could not be flown. Bhanot hid passports of the passengers so the hijackers could not determine passenger nationalities. She also managed to open the emergency door assisting a number of passengers to escape from the plane. Although she could have been the first to jump out of the aircraft and flee to safety when she opened the door, she decided not to and instead started helping the other passengers to escape. As the hijackers opened fire on passengers and crew, Neerja Bhanot lost her life shielding three children from bullets, just 2 days before her 23rd birthday.

Awards and honours

  • Ashoka Chakra, 1987, India
  • Tamgha-e-Pakistan, 1987, (for showing incredible human kindness), Pakistan
  • Flight Safety Foundation Heroism Award 1987, United State
  • Justice for Crimes Award 2005, United States Attorney's Office for the District of Columbia, United States
  • Special Courage Award 2006, United States Department of Justice, United States
  • Civil Aviation Ministry Award 2011, India
  • Bharat Gaurav Award presented at the House of Commons, UK Parliament on 2 July 2016
  • In 2016 a film entitled "Neerja" was released, portraying her herioc life.

"Her loyalties to the passengers of the aircraft in distress will forever be a lasting tribute to the finest qualities of the human spirit"
